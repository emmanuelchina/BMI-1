Limitation of BMI

Although BMI is often a practical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suited for every person. Specific groups should carefully consider their BMI outcomes, and in certain cases, the measurement may not be beneficial to use

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. Consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating their BMI

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss may cause BMI to underestimate body fat content.

Muscle

BMI may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat

Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to their growing baby.Maintabining a healthy pre-pregnancy BMI is advisable to minimise health risks for both mother and child.

Race

Certain health concerns may affect individuals of some origins at lower BMIs than others.To learnn more,it is advised to discuss this with GP or practice nurse.
